The development of computers

In an 1886 letter, Charles Sanders Peirce described how logical operations could be carried out by electrical switching circuits.During 1880-81 he showed that NOR gates alone (or NAND gates alone) can be used to reproduce the functions of all the other logic gates, but this work on it was unpublished until 1933.The first published proof was by Henry M. Sheffer in 1913, so the NAND logical operation is sometimes called Sheffer stroke; the logical NOR is sometimes called Peirce's arrow.Consequently, these gates are sometimes called universal logic gates Eventually, vacuum tubes replaced relays for logic operations. Lee De Forest's modification, in 1907, of the Fleming valve can be used as a logic gate. Ludwig Wittgenstein introduced a version of the 16-row truth table as proposition 5.101 of Tractatus Logico-Philosophicus (1921). Walther Bothe, inventor of the coincidence circuit, got part of the 1954 Nobel Prize in physics, for the first modern electronic AND gate in 1924. Konrad Zuse designed and built electromechanical logic gates for his computer Z1 (from 1935 to 1938). The first recorded idea of using digital electronics for computing was the 1931 paper "The Use of Thyratrons for High Speed Automatic Counting of Physical Phenomena" by C. E. Wynn-Williams.From 1934 to 1936, NEC engineer Akira Nakashima, Claude Shannon, and Victor Shestakov published papers introducing switching circuit theory, using digital electronics for Boolean algebraic operations. In 1936 Alan Turing published his seminal paper On Computable Numbers, with an Application to the Entscheidungsproblem in which he modeled computation in terms of a one-dimensional storage tape, leading to the idea of the Universal Turing machine and Turing-complete systems. The first digital electronic computer was developed in the period April 1936 - June 1939, in the IBM Patent Department, Endicott, New York by Arthur Halsey Dickinson.In this computer IBM introduced, a calculating device with a keyboard, processor and electronic output (display). The competitor to IBM was the digital electronic computer NCR3566, developed in NCR, Dayton, Ohio by Joseph Desch and Robert Mumma in the period April 1939 - August 1939.The IBM and NCR machines were decimal, executing addition and subtraction in binary position code.

The contribution of computers in our lives

  1. Email and Messaging Computers enable instant communication through email and messaging platforms, facilitating quick and efficient information exchange.
    2. Social Media Platforms like Facebook, Twitter, and Instagram connect people globally, fostering communication and networking.
  2. Information Access Internet Computers provide access to a vast repository of information on the internet, allowing users to learn, research, and stay updated on various topics. Search Engines Search engines like Google help users find specific information quickly and easily.
  3. Education Online Learning Computers have transformed education through online courses, making learning accessible to people worldwide. Research Researchers use computers for data analysis, simulations, and collaboration, advancing scientific and academic endeavors.
  4. Work and Business Productivity Tools Computers are integral to businesses, supporting various software tools and applications that enhance productivity. Communication and Collaboration Computers facilitate communication within organizations and enable collaboration among teams, even if they are geographically dispersed.
  5. Media Consumption Computers serve as entertainment hubs for streaming music, videos, and games, providing a diverse range of recreational activities. Creation and Editing Content creation tools on computers allow individuals to produce music, videos, art, and other creative works.
  6. E-commerce Computers drive online shopping and e-commerce platforms, playing a significant role in the global economy. Financial Transactions Banking and financial systems rely heavily on computers for secure and efficient transactions.
  7. Research and Development Computers are instrumental in innovation, supporting research and development across various fields. Technology Advancements Technological breakthroughs and advancements are often driven by computer-based research and experimentation.
